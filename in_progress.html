• NIRO Educational Bot

  • Research Project — supported by NSU Intelligent Robotics (NIRO) Lab

  • October 2024 — Present

  • Description: At the NSU Intelligent Robotics Lab, we are developing the NIRO EDUCATIONAL Bot to facilitate research and provide students with hands-on robotics learning. This robot features built-in elements such as a gyroscope, IR sensors, motors, and WiFi, and combines Raspberry Pi and Arduino for processing, while allowing users to add hardware like LiDAR and depth cameras. Its custom 3D-printed design and PCB, along with a differential drive system, make it highly adaptable to different user requirements.

  • Project Hex

  • Research Project — supported by NSU Intelligent Robotics (NIRO) Lab

  • February 2025 — Present

  • Description: Wheeled robots work well on smooth, flat surfaces but face challenges on rough, uneven terrain, limiting their effectiveness in rescue and exploration missions. Hexapod robots, with six legs each having 3 degrees of freedom, offer greater stability and adaptability in complex environments by distributing weight and navigating obstacles more effectively. Our hexapod is powered by 18 high-torque bus servo motors for precise leg movement, fueled by a LiPo battery for extended operation, and controlled by an Arduino microcontroller for real-time processing. It is also designed modularly to support the addition of advanced components like Raspberry Pi, Lidar, and depth sensors, enhancing its autonomous exploration and environmental mapping capabilities.

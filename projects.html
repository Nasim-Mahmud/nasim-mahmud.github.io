  • Shouhardo: A Web-Based Solution for Improving Hospital Accessibility and Transparency Through User-Centered Design during Covid-19

  • CSE498R: Directed Research

  • Fall 2021

  • Description: A qualitative study during the later phase of COVID-19 with 32 participants revealed major challenges for patients, including unclear hospital information, inflated medicine costs, and difficulty finding the right doctor. To address these issues, we developed an HCI-based multilingual web platform that delivers accurate guidance, service details, and doctor availability, enhancing accessibility and empowering patients to make informed healthcare decisions.

    Tech used: HTML, Vanilla CSS, WordPress

  • Assist Seniors and Disabled with Hand Gesture Recognition

  • CSE299: Junior Design Course, North South University

  • Spring 2020

  • Description: The goal of this project is to implement a low-cost, easily-affordable, and user-friendly hand gesture recognition system to help the elderly and disabled who are incapable of to move or use voice to communicate. The system will employ a phone-based app that enables users to call for assistance while standing far away by making a hand motion.

    Tech used: Python, OpenCV, Raspberry Pi 3B+, Pi-camera, Firebase, Android Studio
